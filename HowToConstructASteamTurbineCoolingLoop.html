How to Construct a Steam Turbine Cooling Loop

Steam Turbines are the most powerful heat removal option available. They remove heat by taking up to 2 kg per second of steam as an input, then outputting the same quantity as water. This temperature reduction of the output represents heat removal. Because the quantity of steam and water is equivalent, you can set this up as a closed loop by creating a small room full of water to heat up for the steam turbine to draw from.

This tutorial will demonstrate how to set up a cooling system for steam turbines.

Prerequisites

Steam turbines and other necessary machines require refined materials. The minimum requirements to set up this cooling system are as follows:

  • 1200 kg of Steel
  • 200 kg of Plastic
  • Renewable Energy technology (for Steam Turbine)
  • Liquid Tuning technology (for Thermo Aquatuner)
  • A duplicant with the Electrical Engineering skill

Automation is also strongly recommended. This tutorial includes steps to implement automation and assumes you will implement it in your build. The automation requirements are as follows:

  • Supply of refined metal (Lead is recommended)
  • Liquid Tuning technology (for Liquid Pipe Thermo Sensor)
  • Smart Home technology (for Automation Wire)

1. Construct a Water Tank

The water tank will hold the water and steam for the steam turbine to function. Note that this will be a fully enclosed room that is inaccessible once complete.

Start by constructing a room that is five tiles wide with walls on the side that are two tiles high, all using insulated tile.

Fill this room with water. You can do this with bottle emptiers.You need to fill this tank to the point where water reaches the second tile of the tank height. This pushes out any stray gas, which will allow the tank to contain just steam or water. You need to empty 5200 kg of water into the tank to accomplish this.

Optionally, you can instead use 200 kg of two different types of water. One type of water will sit on the tile on top of the other, effectively reaching the required height and pushing out the gas.

The other types of water you can use are polluted water, salt water, and brine. Salt water or brine is recommended because these all drop debris when vaporized, and the dirt from polluted water can cook into a solid tile of sand at a hot enough temperature.

2. Add a Thermo Aquatuner

The Thermo Aquatuner will be the source of heat for the water tank. It takes a liquid input and transfers a large amount of heat from the liquid to the machine itself, which then radiates it into the surrounding environment. This will vaporize the water into steam, which is then fed into the steam turbine to begin the heat removal process.

Construct a thermo aquatuner on the left side of the tank. You must use steel as the material. Any other metal will cause the aquatuner to overheat.

Add input and output pipes. Also place a liquid pipe bridge after the input and connect it to the input and output lines of the aquatuner. This will allow for liquid flow to continue when the aquatuner is either full of liquid or turned off.

Place a liquid pipe thermo sensor on a pipe segment feeding into the aquatuner input outside the water tank and use automation wire to connect it to the aquatuner.

Set the sensor to send a green signal at temperatures above 20 degrees C. This will prevent the aquatuner from freezing the liquid in the pipes.

Complete the pipe loop by constructing pipes leading to the areas you want to cool and adding in a liquid reservoir as a buffer. Using either a liquid pump or bottle drainer, fill the pipe loop with polluted water until the reservoir has a constant 100 kg of polluted water. If polluted water is unavailable, you can use clean water instead. Clean water has a higher freezing point, and is slightly more dangerous to use.

Finally, set up the power connection for the aquatuner. Because it consumes 1200 kW, you must use conductive wire drawing power from a large power transformer. You don't want the aquatuner running just yet, so leave one segment of wire disconnected.

3. Construct a Steam Turbine

The Steam Turbine is the final piece of the loop, acting as your source of cooling. As the thermo aquatuner vaporizes the water in the tank, the steam turbine will consume the steam and reintroduce it to the tank as cooler water.

At this point, you're almost ready to enclose the water tank. Make sure anything within the tank is properly set up because any further changes inside become very difficult once the system is sealed and running.

Begin by placing a steam turbine one tile above the top of the tank. You can't construct this yet because it's not sitting on solid tile. But you can use the construction command as reference for the pipes to place.

Construct a liquid vent in the water tank on the upper tile of the far right side. This should be in line with the output of the steam turbine.

Now you can add pipes from the steam turbine to the water tank. While you can build a straight connection from the turbine to the vent, the line we'll build here lets the output water collect heat from around the steam turbine to prevent it from overheating.

Construct two lines of radiant pipes, one line leading from the steam turbine output to the far left side of the turbine and the other line at the bottom of the steam turbine two tiles below. Connect these on the far left side with a liquid pipe bridge, and then connect the bottom right pipe segment with the liquid vent in the tank below.

You might not be able to construct some of these pipe segments because they're out of reach. Like the steam turbine, you can leave these queued. The only parts you must construct before continuing are the liquid vent and the pipe segment within the tank.

Once those parts are constructed, you can now seal the tank. Construct a line of insulated tiles on top of the tank.

Once sealed, the water tank should appear to be completely full of liquid. If you have any gas trapped in the tank, deconstruct the tile above the gas and reconstruct it. This should force out the gas. If that doesn't work, use a bottle emptier to add a little more water before reconstructing the tile.

At this point, all remaining construction commands queued are available to complete. Wait for your duplicants to finish building everything. Once complete, you can turn on the system by connecting the break in the wire you left earlier.

4. Observing the System in Action

Cooling begins as soon as the thermo aquatuner turns on. You can observe this by looking at the temperature of the pipe contents before and after entering the aquatuner.

The steam turbine will take some time to fully activate. The water in the tank must first vaporize into steam, then increase in temperature to 125 degrees C. At that point you can observe the steam turbine activating, consuming steam, and outputting water back into the tank.

As long as the steam in your tank remains below 135 degrees C, this cooling loop runs indefinitely. Both the turbine and aquatuner might occasionally shut off as temperatures of the steam and polluted water drop too low, but they will turn on again as more heat is added to the system.

Where to Go From Here

This steam turbine cooling loop forms the basis of most of your cooling needs going forward. While you can use this setup for almost any of your cooling needs, here are a few additional options to consider.

Additional Steam Turbines and Thermo Aquatuners

You can expand your water tank to make use of multiple steam turbines and thermo aquatuners. This is a good option if you want to transfer a great deal of heat over a large area through your cooling loop.

If you are using polluted water as your cooling loop contents, two steam turbines can support three thermo aquatuners. Other liquids might use a different ratio.

Radiant Pipes Instead of Thermo Aquatuners

Because thermo aquatuners use a lot of power, you might consider transferring heat into the water tank using radiant pipes instead. This option works, but with important caveats.

The primary consideration is that this loop will only cool temperatures to a minimum of 125 degrees C, as the steam turbine can't turn on at temperatures below that. This means that any machines in the area you are cooling should be constructed from heat-resistant materials such as steel.

The other consideration is that you must use a different liquid as a coolant, as polluted water vaporizes at about 120 degrees C. This leaves either crude oil or petroleum as the only viable options. Because their specific heat capacities are substantially lower than polluted water, they also won't transfer heat as fast.

If these are acceptable tradeoffs for your setup, then you can use radiant pipes.

Steam Turbine Cooling

This tutorial provides some basic cooling for the steam turbine, but also left space for additional heating. You can expand your cooling loop to run through the middle of the steam turbine for additional cooling.

Additionally, the gas in the atmosphere around the steam turbine can affect cooling efficacy. The best gas to use for this is hydrogen. You can encase the steam turbine within insulated tile and pump hydrogen into the room for the most effective cooling.

Cold Injector

A cold injector is an optional augmentation that allows you to more precisely control temperatures. It works by confining the cooling loop to a small area, then automating a nearby airlock door to control when heat is moved into a nearby zone. The other side of this door can either act as the area you want to cool or as a cool zone for a separate cooling loop.

Refer to the cold injector guide for information on setting this up.

Space Materials

After you start exploring space with rockets, you can begin to craft thermium and super coolant. Both of these tremendously improve the cooling efficacy of your setup.

Super coolant is a liquid that can hold and transfer substantially more heat than polluted water. Using this as your pipe liquid substantially increases the rate the loop cools. It also has an extremely low freezing point, allowing you to cool atmospheres to sub-zero temperatures.

Thermium is a refined metal with an extremely high melting point and thermal conductivity. Constructing a thermo aquatuner out of thermium increases the rate at which heat is transferred into the water tank.

If you want to prioritize obtaining one material, super coolant is more effective for cooling purposes.
